First public build of **Accessible Media Editor** — an accessible audio/video
cutter for blind and visually impaired users, built with wxPython and embedded
FFmpeg. Fully keyboard-driven and NVDA-friendly.

**What you can do in this version**
- Open one audio or video file and mark keep / discard regions on the timeline.
- Listen while you edit: play, montage (skip discarded parts), verify a cut,
  scrub, and jump between detected silences (handy for ad breaks).
- Export either **one re-joined file** (discarded regions removed) or
  **N separate files** (one per kept region).
- Copy (stream, near-instant) or re-encode export, with per-format settings.
- English and French, with speech feedback independent of the screen reader.

This is an early seed (0.1.0). The editor was carried over, working, from the
Accessible Media Converter cutter; a real multi-file timeline, fades and effects
are planned for future versions.
